About the Library

The College Library was established in 1949 and is easily accessible to all students and staff. Starting with just 2,108 books, our collection has grown to over 57,795 volumes. We subscribe to numerous national periodicals, journals, and newspapers, and provide access to a vast array of e-journals and databases through the UGC-INFLIBNET N-LIST program.

Spanning 2,179.6 sq. mts., the library offers a seating capacity for 200 readers and is equipped with 12 computer systems with internet, fully automated with Koha software. Our digital section contains numerous CDs, DVDs, and video cassettes.

Services & Facilities

  • Web OPAC: Our Online Public Access Catalogue allows users to search the library's collection 24/7 through the Koha software.
  • Automation: Circulation services (issue, return, renewal) are fully automated using smart ID cards with barcodes, eliminating long queues.
  • E-Resources: Access to thousands of e-journals and e-books via the INFLIBNET (N-LIST) Programme and EBSCO-Host database.
  • Computer Access: 12 dedicated computer systems are available for students and faculty for academic purposes.
  • Reprography Service: Photocopying facilities are available within the library.
  • Institutional Repository: We maintain a collection of college news clippings, prospectuses, magazines, and other institutional publications.
  • Circulation Policy: Special borrowing provisions for meritorious students and during examination periods, with SMS alerts for due dates.
  • Accessibility: Full assistance is provided by library staff to physically challenged persons.
